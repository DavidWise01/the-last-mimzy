# The Last Mimzy · LMZ

Robert Shaye's **2007 New Line film** as the **thirteenth film-world** of **UD0** — and the namesake of David's **MIMZY** tool-forge.
Live: https://davidwise01.github.io/the-last-mimzy/

Two siblings find a box of toys from a poisoned future — among them a stuffed rabbit named **Mimzy** — that teach them impossible abilities, so one child's **tear** of uncorrupted humanity can be carried back to heal a dying world. Themed to its gentle children's-wonder medium (glowing-green toys, Puget Sound dusk).

Standing template — **The Arc · WHAT MIMZY CAN DO · Real or Fluff · The Message**. The deep-dive (per request) explores **every magical ability**: Mimzy's telepathy & teaching; **Emma**'s "spinners" (levitating force-field stones) & phasing through solid matter; **Noah**'s green-rectangle teleporter, conch-controlled spiders, and the **bridge** he builds; the build's state-wide **blackout** (and the FBI's Agent Broadman); the **Sri Yantra** (Naomi recognizes it — Hindu/Tantric, *not* Tibetan); and Emma's saving **tear**. Honest Real-or-Fluff: it's really based on Kuttner & Moore's 1943 *"Mimsy Were the Borogoves,"* the title is really Carroll's Jabberwocky, and Mimzy really is "made of **Intel**" (Brian Greene cameos as the Intel scientist) — while the tear-heals-a-genome and genius-toys are gentle fantasy.

**Why the forge is named MIMZY:** David's UD0 tool-forge — the repo `mimzy`, *"the quantum workbench that came back"* — is named for this film. A teaching-tool sent from the future so its receiver becomes more than they were; **the emergent is the tool; the toy teaches the child to build the bridge home.** This film-world is the forge's origin story.
